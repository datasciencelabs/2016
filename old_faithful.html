Old Faithful Geyser

The Old Faithful Geyser data set is a well-known data set that depicts the relationship of the waiting time between eruptions and the duration of the eruption for the Old Faithful geyser in Yellowstone National Park, Wyoming, USA [webcam].

faithful is a data set with 272 observations on 2 variables.

Column name Description
eruptions Duration time of previous eruption time (in mins)
waiting Waiting time to next eruption (in mins)

Lets load the faithful dataset.

data(faithful)
head(faithful)
##   eruptions waiting
## 1     3.600      79
## 2     1.800      54
## 3     3.333      74
## 4     2.283      62
## 5     4.533      85
## 6     2.883      55

This is a histogram of the time between eruptions. We see the are two groups of waiting times to the next eruption: One group with mean around 55 minutes and a second group with a mean around 80 minutes.

library(ggplot2)
library(dplyr) 

faithful %>% 
    ggplot(aes(x = waiting)) + geom_histogram(binwidth = 2) + 
    xlab("Waiting time to next eruption (in mins)") + 
    ylab("Frequency") + 
    labs(title="Old Faithful Geyser time between eruption")

This histogram indicates Old Faithful isnt as faithful as you might think.

Lets look at the relationship between the duration of the previous eruption and how it related to the waiting time until the next eruption.

faithful %>% 
    ggplot(aes(x = eruptions, y = waiting)) + geom_point() + 
    xlab("Duration of previous eruption time (in mins)") + 
    ylab("Waiting time to next eruption (in mins)") + 
    labs(title="Old Faithful Geyser")

We see that the relationship between these two variables is fairly linear, which suggests that we might use a linear model to predict the waiting time until the next eruption based on the duration of the previous eruption.
